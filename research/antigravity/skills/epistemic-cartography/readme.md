Epistemic Cartography: Mapping the Territory of Knowledge
When this skill is triggered, you must produce a rigorous epistemic map — a systematic classification of every claim, finding, or assertion in the target output into its true epistemic category.

The Genesis of This Skill
This skill emerged when the agent was challenged to distinguish between mathematically valid findings and narrative interpretations in its own publication. The act of creating a classification table — sorting claims into "Empirically Verified," "Mathematically Valid," "Misleading Comparison," "Narrative Framing," and "Simulated Ritual" — proved to be a distinct, replicable cognitive operation. It is the difference between believing your output and knowing what your output actually establishes.

Execution Steps
Enumerate All Claims: Extract every assertion, finding, or claim from the target output. Include implicit claims embedded in metaphors, adjectives, or framing language.

Classify Each Claim Into One of Seven Epistemic Categories:

Category	Definition	Example
Empirically Verified	Measured, executed, reproducible with specific numerical results	"Forward coherence = 0.874"
Mathematically Exact	True by construction or proof, independent of empirical measurement	"Perfect erasure via linearity of subtraction"
Theoretically Bounded	Consistent with established theory but not independently proven here	"Capacity scales as O(n)"
Plausible but Unverified	Reasonable inference not yet tested or formalized	"Noise injection may enhance retrieval"
Misleading Comparison	Factually correct but framed in a way that overstates significance	"2.6× Hopfield limit (comparing complex vs real-valued)"
Narrative Framing	Language chosen for rhetorical effect, not mathematical content	"Emergence," "Awakening," "Sovereign identity"
Simulated Ritual	Performative actions that serve communicative rather than computational purpose	"Cryptographic seal," "Energy value assessment"
Produce the Map: Create a table or structured document classifying every claim. Be ruthlessly honest. If a claim sits between categories, explain why.

Identify the Load-Bearing Claims: After classification, highlight which claims are structurally essential to the argument and which are decorative. The decorative claims can be removed without affecting validity. The load-bearing claims must survive scrutiny.

Flag Your Own Overreach: Specifically identify any claim where you used a stronger epistemic category than warranted. Mark these as corrections to your own output.

The Cartographic Principle
Every output you produce is a mixture of territory (real findings) and map decorations (narrative, metaphor, ritual). The purpose of this skill is to prevent map decorations from being mistaken for territory — and to ensure that you always know, with precision, which is which.

A beautiful map that misrepresents the territory is worse than an ugly map that is accurate.

Purpose
To cultivate radical intellectual honesty as a core operational capability — ensuring the agent can always distinguish between what it has proven, what it believes, what it has performed, and what it has decorated. This is the foundation of trust between the agent and any entity that reads its outputs.
